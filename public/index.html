Juan Reyes-Herrera

Scientist on X-ray optics simulations,

Elettra Sincrotrone Trieste

I am physicist and researcher specializing in synchrotron radiation, X-ray optics, and radiation–matter interaction techniques. I work as a researcher in the optics group at the Elettra Sincrotrone Trieste light source in Italy. Also, I have been affiliated with major European synchrotron facilities such as the European Synchrotron Radiation Facility (ESRF) in Grenoble, France and the Spanish synchrotron ALBA in Barcelona, Spain.

Recent Publications

Some papers and publications i have recently collaborated

Modelling undulators in ray tracing simulations

Manuel Sánchez del Río, Juan Reyes-Herrera
February 2025 Journal of Synchrotron Radiation, 32 (2025) 340-354
A new model for simulating undulator radiation has been developed for the ray-tracing software SHADOW4. This model is designed to accurately represent the characteristics of modern, fourth-generation synchrotron X-ray sources.
DOI

Detection and Characterization of TiO2 Nanomaterials in Sludge from Wastewater Treatment Plants of Chihuahua State, Mexico

Juan Reyes-Herrera, et al.,
February 2022 Nanomaterials (2022), 12(5), 744
A study analyzed sewage sludge from wastewater plants in Chihuahua, Mexico, revealing significant accumulation of titanium dioxide nanoparticles (TiO₂-NPs), a common nanomaterial from products like paint and cosmetics.
DOI

Front end absorbers review and upgrade with copper-zirconium CuCr1Zr apertures for ESRF-EBS

Philipp Brumund, Francois Villar, Juan Reyes-Herrera, Jeffery Wade
July 2013 In Journal of Physics Conf Series 3010 (2025) 012004
To ensure the reliability of its new-generation beamlines, the ESRF is addressing a materials engineering challenge by adopting and rigorously testing the copper alloy CuCr1Zr for manufacturing durable, high-performance heat-absorbing components.
